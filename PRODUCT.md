# Product

## Register

brand

## Users
Global music producers and audio engineers (English-speaking, not Israeli market). They arrive from IG/social funnels or search, skeptical of plugin hype, often mid-session in a DAW. Job to be done: decide fast whether RevLimiter (and the wider plugin line) sounds good and is worth trying — then get the trial or buy with zero friction.

## Product Purpose
Marketing site for Revaudio, a boutique VST plugin venture ("plugins built like cars"). Flagship: RevLimiter, a mastering limiter in paid founding-tester beta ($93 launch via Lemon Squeezy). The site's job: sell a sound and a feeling on top, prove technical credibility underneath, and route visitors to the demo/trial funnel. Success = trial registrations and checkouts.

## Brand Personality
Honest-nerdy, tactile, mechanical. Voice: Valhalla-style honesty + the car/garage metaphor as character + FabFilter-grade technical proof. Emotions: the confidence of a well-built machine; delight in physical props (gauges, doors, cranes, license plates) that actually communicate state.

## Anti-references
- Universal-Audio heritage swagger (no legend to borrow — the heritage is a metaphor).
- Baby-Audio hype density ("the future sound of…").
- Any plugin-marketing cliché: "unleash", "take your sound to the next level", "pristine", fake scarcity, fake testimonials.
- Generic SaaS landing patterns (hero-metric template, identical card grids). This site is a dark garage, not a dashboard.

## Design Principles
- The metaphor orients, plain language instructs: car terms for feel and headlines, technical terms for what a control does.
- Props must communicate state, not just decorate (the door lights, the stamp slams, the sway stops when mounted).
- Honesty is the moat: no invented quotes, no staged demos, no fake urgency. One fake-sounding element costs more than it gains.
- Photoreal physical assets with live text over blank regions — the house recipe; no baked type.
- One or two live metaphors per section; the rest quiet and disciplined.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA is the bar (EAA microenterprise-exempt; risk profile is US nuisance letters). Reduced-motion alternatives for every animation. Real semantics on interactive elements; state changes announced (aria-live). Keyboard focus always visible.
